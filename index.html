Striped Skunk

The striped skunk is a stoutly-built, short-limbed animal with a small, conical head and a long, heavily furred tail.

  • Scientific Name: Mephitis mephitis
  • Average Length: 64.5 cm
  • Average Lifespan: 3 years (wild), 10 years (captive)
  • Habitat: mixed woodlands

A polygamous omnivore with few natural predators (save for birds of prey), the striped skunk has a long history of association with humans, having been trapped and captively bred for its fur and kept as an exotic pet.
